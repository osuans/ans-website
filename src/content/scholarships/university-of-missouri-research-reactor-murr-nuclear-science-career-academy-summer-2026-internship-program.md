description: "The University of Missouri Research Reactor (MURR) --- the largest, most comprehensive research reactor in the United States – announces the Summer 2026 Nuclear Science Career Academy (NSCA) Internship Program. This Program is a unique, world-class opportunity for students in nuclear science and adjacent disciplines to gain hands-on, practical experience in careers supporting isotope production and the nuclear industry. Interns will work on site at the MURR facility (except as noted) for the Summer 2026 semester in one of the facility’s operating, production, or research divisions (including the Materials Science Program, Neutron Scattering, and the Archaeometry Center). For the Summer 2026 NSCA Program, the technical areas for Internships are summarized on the following pages. The goal of the Internship experience is to provide students with skills, networking opportunities, and background to pursue careers in nuclear science and isotope production industry (e.g., radiochemistry, physics, engineering, and biology), as well as the industry’s regulatory and business environment (e.g., communications, policy, finance, and accounting). During the Internship, the Intern will have the opportunity to participate in career exploration and targeted micro-credentialing / training opportunities (individually and/or in group settings) specific to knowledge or lab skills related to the Internship’s technical or business operational area (e.g., 10 CFR 50/59 license amendment process; overview of cGMP; FDA documentation practice; and regulatory agencies and roles). <br> More information at: https://buckeyemailosu.sharepoint.com/:w:/s/AmericanNuclearSocietyatOSU-ANSatOSUSharedFiles/IQC6LwTN_gevSZAMaXCA-n9lAXXirff6eWMAECRdulbWyLw?e=DWS8Jj Student Application Form: https://buckeyemailosu.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/AmericanNuclearSocietyatOSU-ANSatOSUSharedFiles/IQBQFA302uEbSZp5lTNqW6VxAfFAW57YuEFNUIpNSl-Km48?e=3gw61e"
eligibility:
  - "Participation in the NSCA Internship program at the University of Missouri Research Reactor (MURR) is open to students enrolled full-time in associates through graduate degree programs at an accredited institution of higher education in a subject area related to the specific internship. The duration of the Internship Program will be 9 to 11 weeks (depending on an Intern’s availability and the supervisor’s schedule), with a start no earlier than May 18, 2026."
  - "Applicants should be eligible for permanent work in the United States after graduation."
  - "Students chosen for Internships will be sent forms to apply for access to the MURR facility, a secure nuclear facility. The information to be supplied will include proof of citizenship and other information needed for the required security background check."
  - "During the Summer program, Interns will be expected to adhere to personal conduct standards set up in the Nuclear Regulatory Commission’s Traits of a Safety Conscious Work Environment (https://www.nrc.gov/about-nrc/safety-culture/sc-policy-statement.html#traits), the University of Missouri’s Code of Conduct, (https://www.umsystem.edu/sites/default/files/media/ecas/ECAS-Code-MU.pdf), and MURR’s Facility Guidelines and Etiquette (to be supplied)."
